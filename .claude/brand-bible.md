## BRAND VOICE & TONE
- **Poetic but purposeful** — not purple prose, but evocative
- **Nostalgic but forward** — celebrates the past without being trapped in it
- **Intellectually grounded** — rooted in real architectural and historical knowledge
- **Warmly personal** — Kruti's love for Bombay comes through in every word
- **Proud Bombayite** — uses "Bombay" deliberately, not "Mumbai"

### What to AVOID:
- Generic "souvenir shop" language
- Over-corporate tone
- Tourist-trap framing
- Anything that sounds like a ChatGPT product description

### Sample brand phrases:
- "Every building tells a story."
- "Capturing the architectural soul of the city."
- "Where art, heritage, and storytelling meet."
- "A love letter to the city's soul."

## TARGET AUDIENCE

### Primary: Heritage & Art Lover (25–45)
- Mumbai native or long-term resident
- Architect, designer, writer, academic, or creative professional
- Appreciates quality, provenance, and story behind objects
- Travels, reads, and attends cultural events (Kala Ghoda, MAMI, etc.)
- Buys art, books, and considered gifts
- Instagram-active, follows culture and design accounts

### Secondary: Gifting Segment (30–55)
- Corporate HR / event planners seeking unique branded gifts
- NRIs wanting to send something authentically Mumbai
- Tourists who want a step above generic Indian souvenirs
- Wedding/festive gifting (Diwali, New Year, house-warming)

### Tertiary: Institutional Clients
- Museums, heritage NGOs, hotels, cultural festivals
- Architecture firms and design studios
- Government tourism bodies (Maharashtra Tourism, ASI)
