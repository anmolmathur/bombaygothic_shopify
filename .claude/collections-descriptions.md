## The 4 Architectural Cityscapes (base illustrations)

1. **Kala Ghoda Kaleidoscope** — Watson's Hotel, Army & Navy Building, David Sassoon Library, and Elphinstone College.
2. **Victorian Gothic Ensemble** — A continuous stretch along the Esplanade: Old Secretariat, University of Mumbai, Rajabai Clock Tower, and the Bombay High Court.
3. **Urbs Prima in Indis** — Victoria Terminus (CSMT), BMC Headquarters, and the Times of India Building.
4. **The Gateway to India** — Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, Gateway of India, Prince of Wales Museum (CSMVS), and the Royal Alfred Sailors' Home.

## Design Series 1 — Bombay Bohemian

Bombay Bohemian reflects a sense of freedom, creativity, and individuality that has long defined Bombay and finds resonance in its evolving urban fabric. This series presents a vibrant and eclectic visual language that mirrors the city's energy and bohemian character.

## Design Series 2 — Where Lines Meet Colour

Where Lines Meet Colour brings together historic architectural illustrations of Bombay with a contemporary, graphic sensibility. The series is defined by an architectural line drawing base punctuated with carefully placed pops of colour, unconstrained by formal artistic boundaries. By blending the city's rich architectural legacy with the vibrancy of pop colours, this series creates thoughtful, stylish products that seamlessly integrate art into everyday life.

## Design Series 3 — Starry Starry Night

Inspired from Vincent van Gogh's The Starry Night, reimagined through the lens of Bombay's winter evenings under clear, starlit skies. Set against deep midnight blues evoking a sense of cool sea breezes, softly lit heritage buildings, and quiet streets — the collection captures the magic of Kala Ghoda after dark. Calm, nostalgic, and quietly festive, this collection celebrates Bombay's mood — where warmth comes not from fireplaces, but from architecture, light, and memory.

## Design Series 4 — The Lancet Line

Inspired by Gothic lancet windows and Bombay's historic facades, The Lancet Line is a study in structure and restraint. Hand-sketched illustrations explore rhythm, order, and proportion through a linear, monochrome lens — where architectural language reimagines everyday objects as quiet, considered design statements and quiet conversation pieces.
